# Key Engineering Decisions

## Breadboards Rejected

The decision was made to avoid solderless breadboards for the motor-control platform.

Reasoning:
- reduced reliability
- poor mechanical stability
- increased risk of intermittent electrical faults
- unsuitable for long-term robotics integration

The project will instead use solderable prototyping boards and structured wiring.

---

## Industrial Power Infrastructure Selected

The Mean Well LRS-150-12 was selected as the primary 12V system power supply.

Reasoning:
- industrial-grade reliability
- sufficient current headroom
- scalable for future multi-axis expansion
- reduced risk of unstable motor behavior caused by weak power infrastructure

---

## Single-Axis Bring-Up Strategy

The project will begin with single-axis validation before attempting full robotic arm integration.

Initial objective:
- reliable motion control using one motor, one driver, and one embedded controller

Reasoning:
- isolate debugging variables
- simplify power integration
- simplify firmware validation
- establish repeatable motion infrastructure before scaling

# Technical Concepts Studied

## A4988 Architecture

Reviewed:
- STEP input
- DIR input
- VMOT power input
- VDD logic power
- current limiting
- common ground requirements
- motor coil outputs

Key understanding:
- STEP pulses generate movement
- DIR controls rotation direction
- incorrect current limiting may damage hardware
